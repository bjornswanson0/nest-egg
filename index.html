How the math works
  • Everything compounds monthly; contributions land before growth each month.
  • Employer match is applied per paycheck up to your match cap — no year-end true-up.
  • Annual contribution limits (401(k), IRA, HSA) cap contributions within each year; the blocked dollars keep working elsewhere.
  • Extra debt payments go to the highest-APR balance first (avalanche). When a debt is paid off, its old minimum payment rolls into your plan instead of disappearing.
  • The recommended order re-allocates the same monthly budget: employer match → debt at or above your high-interest threshold → emergency fund → HSA → Roth IRA → 401(k) to the annual max → remaining debt → savings overflow. Your plan and the recommended plan always spend identical dollars, so the gap between the two lines is pure ordering.
  • Your nest-egg goal is monthly spending × 12, inflated to retirement age, divided by your withdrawal rate (the "4% rule" by default).
  • Taxes aren't modeled — pre-tax and Roth dollars are both taken at face value, so treat results as directional, not gospel.

This is an educational tool, not financial advice. Numbers are projections built on your assumptions — real markets won't cooperate so smoothly.
